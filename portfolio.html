Portfolio

Graphic Design and Illustrative works

"5-Second Sumo"

5-Second Sumo is a mobile application where the player must throw food off of a moving conveyer belt to feed a hungry sumo wrestler without having food stay on the floor for longer than 5 seconds. The game is essentially based off of the “5-second rule” where food that hit the floor is edible as long as it is picked up within 5 seconds.

"Cheez-o-Rama"

Cheez-o-Rama is a three part identity branding project based on a fictional festival commemorating cheese and other cheese products. Cheez-o-Rama includes: an eight-fold brochure, a promotional poster, and three different types of packaging samples.

"UVSA & VAHSA Camp Gear"

UVSA (The Union of Vietnamese Students Associations) Camp and VAHSA (Vietnamese American High School Alliance) Camp are summer leadership camps for college students and high school students, respectively. Each of the hundred campers receive a t-shirt, lanyard, name badge, and drawstring bag emblazened with a design of the yearly new mascot and motto.
"The Kidney Thief"

The Kidney Thief is a digital illustration project in reference to an urban legend about a person who knocks out their victims, and harvests their kidneys as they are left unconscious in a bathtub of ice. This piece was featured in Cal Poly Pomona’s 2D/3D Show, an art show open to student works created in class.
"Ninja Chowdown"

Ninja Chowdown is a pixel based mobile application featuring a ninja rushing through the streets in pursuit of the rival samurai sushi truck. The game is an automatic side scrolling platformer where the player must dodge obstacles, eat junk food, and destroy anything in their path.

"Cyber Security and Awareness Fair"

Based off of the personification of a computer virus and an avatar characterized as a firewall protection system. The banner was commissioned by Cal Poly Pomona’s Stop Think Connect program and was presented on the side of the school’s parking structure to advertise the 2016 Cyber Security and Awareness Fair. The right portion of the design is six feet wide and contains the information for the year’s fair, whereas the left portion of the design is determined to be used for future Cyber Security and Awareness Fairs.
